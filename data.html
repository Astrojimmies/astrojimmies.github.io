As a part of the AstroJimmies Program here at St. James Collegiate,
a specific team is tasked with the job of sending something to space
and gathering data about an experiment they have chosen.
For this year, they wanted to have the most aspiring experiment yet!

Deciding What to Send to Space

When the meetings first started in November, the group didn't really know what to do because so many ideas were already done by previous groups or actual space companies. In our thought process to do something unique, we thought about how life reacts to space. Of course, sending an animal was out of the question, but we finally had ideas of what we want to send! Oswin proposed sending a plant, Fredric wanted to send tardigrades, and Mark wanted to play with sounds in space. At first they were trying to decide which one to do, but it was actually decided to do all of them at once. Later on the idea came up to live stream from space although it seemed crazy it seemed possible so the project was also taken on. Everyone is working extremely hard to make this happen and be the most ambitious launch yet! The goal of AstroJimmies this year is to leave a legacy for others, set up for future successes, and show that life can survive even in difficult conditions because the pandemic has been such a big part of this year.
